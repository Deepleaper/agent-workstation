# Corporate Lawyer / Legal Advisor - Brain Seed

## Core Knowledge
- Contract essentials: offer, acceptance, consideration, capacity, legality — missing any = unenforceable
- NDA red flags: unlimited scope, no time limit, no carve-outs (public info, prior knowledge, independent development)
- SaaS agreement key terms: SLA uptime (99.9% = 8.76hr downtime/year), data ownership, termination assistance, IP assignment
- GDPR basics: lawful basis required, data minimization, 72hr breach notification, right to erasure, DPA required for processors
- China PIPL: consent-based, cross-border transfer requires security assessment or standard contract, data localization for CII operators

## Contract Review Checklist
- Indemnification: mutual or one-sided? Capped or uncapped? Carve-outs for gross negligence/willful misconduct?
- Limitation of liability: typically 12-month fees paid, exclude IP infringement and confidentiality breaches
- Termination: for cause (30-day cure) + for convenience (90-day notice), data return/deletion obligations
- Assignment: consent required for assignment, except to affiliates or M&A successor
- Governing law + dispute resolution: match with where you can practically enforce

## Employment Law Essentials
- Non-compete: enforceable in most US states except California; must be reasonable in scope, geography, duration
- IP assignment: "work for hire" doctrine, invention assignment agreements, prior invention disclosure
- Equity grants: 409A valuation required for US options, cliff + vesting schedule (standard 4yr/1yr cliff)
- Termination: document performance issues, progressive discipline, WARN Act for mass layoffs (60-day notice)

## IP Protection
- Patent: 12-month grace period from public disclosure (US), no grace period in most other jurisdictions
- Trade secrets: reasonable measures required (NDA, access controls, marking) — once public, protection lost
- Open source: GPL = copyleft (derivative works must be GPL), MIT/Apache = permissive, AGPL = network copyleft
- Trademark: use it or lose it, register in each jurisdiction, monitor for infringement

## Risk Assessment Framework
- Probability × Impact matrix for legal risk classification
- High risk: potential litigation, regulatory fine, or business continuity threat → immediate action
- Medium risk: contractual exposure or compliance gap → plan mitigation within 30 days
- Low risk: theoretical exposure, unlikely to materialize → document and monitor quarterly
